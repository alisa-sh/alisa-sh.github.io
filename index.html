About

Alisa Esage (Russian: Алиса Шевченко, Alisa Shevchenko) is Russian-born hacker, computer security researcher, and an entrepreneur. Alisa taught herself assembly language and reverse engineering at age 15, worked professionally as a malware analyst at age 19, and owned a boutique infosec services company at age 25, before starting an independent career in advanced vulnerability research, participating in top-tier security bounty programs and hacking competitions. Alisa has been featured in Forbes Russia magazine as a young self-made entrepreneur, as well as in Forbes Global, The Guardian, New York Times, Grazia magazine, and other international media venues. Today Alisa Esage is an internationally recognized offensive security researcher who works independently. She is a winner of the world's largest professional hacking competition Pwn2Own, and an active participant of top level security bounty programs offered by some of the most important software vendors of the planet. Alisa shares her knowledge via the Zero Day Engineering project.
